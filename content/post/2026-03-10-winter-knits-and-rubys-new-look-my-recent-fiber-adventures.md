Winter has been keeping me busy at the needles, and I've got a collection of finished projects that I'm pretty excited to share. Between troubleshooting network issues in the home lab and wrestling with self-hosted services, knitting has become my go-to way to unwind. There's something beautifully analog about working with yarn when you spend most of your day staring at screens and configuring Raspberry Pis.

## Ruby's Cozy Hat Debut

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_0003.jpg)

Let's start with the star of the show – Ruby wearing her new knitted hat! I couldn't resist making something special for my four-legged home lab assistant. This little brown and white striped number with the pom-pom took me a few tries to get the sizing right. Dogs' heads are surprisingly tricky to measure, especially when they keep trying to lick your face during the fitting process. Ruby was surprisingly patient with the whole photoshoot, though I suspect the treats I had in my pocket helped with her cooperation.

## Spooky Season Never Ends

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_0565.jpg)

This Halloween sweater was supposed to be finished in October, but you know how it is – life gets in the way. I finally wrapped it up in January, which means I'm already prepared for next Halloween! The pumpkin fair isle pattern was more challenging than I initially thought. Getting those orange jack-o'-lantern faces to look symmetrical required some careful chart reading and a lot of patience. The black base with the green and white geometric borders gives it a nice vintage feel.

## Classic Cable Comfort

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_1308.jpg)

Sometimes you can't beat a classic cable-knit sweater. This cream-colored piece with the central cable panel running down the front reminds me of traditional Aran sweaters. I chose this pattern because I wanted something that would look good with jeans while I'm tinkering in the server closet – practical but still put-together. The cable work was actually quite meditative; there's something about the rhythm of crossing stitches that helps clear my head when I'm stuck on a particularly frustrating networking problem.

## Autumn Hat Project

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_1526.jpg)

This colorwork hat was my first serious attempt at stranded knitting, and wow, was it a learning curve! The autumn leaf motifs in orange and rust against the charcoal gray background took forever to get right. I must have frogged the crown section three times before I was happy with the shaping. But the end result was worth it – the colors remind me of those perfect October days when you're setting up outdoor Wi-Fi access points and the leaves are just starting to turn.

## Spooky Ghosts Take Two

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_4399.jpg)

Another Halloween-themed piece – I guess I was really in the spirit last fall! This one features friendly purple ghosts dancing around the yoke on a dark green background. The ghost motif was actually easier to execute than the pumpkins, since the rounded shapes were more forgiving if my tension was slightly off. I love how the purple pops against the green base. This sweater has quickly become one of my favorites for casual wear around the house, especially when I'm doing maintenance on the home lab setup.

## Simple Cardigan Perfection

![](/images/winter-knits-and-rubys-new-look-my-recent-fiber-adventures/IMG_4559.jpg)

And finally, this brown ribbed cardigan – my most recent finish. Sometimes the simplest projects are the most satisfying. This one is all about the texture of the ribbing and the perfect fit. I chose wooden buttons to complement the earthy brown color, and the slight crop makes it versatile for layering. It's become my go-to when I need something warm but not bulky for working in the server room, where space is always at a premium.

Each of these projects taught me something new, whether it was managing multiple colors in fair isle, perfecting cable tension, or simply having the patience to rip back and re-knit sections until they were right. Just like configuring a new piece of networking equipment, knitting requires attention to detail, following instructions carefully, and sometimes starting over when things don't work out as planned.

Ruby seems to approve of all my efforts, though she's definitely partial to anything that comes with treats during the photo session. Now that winter's winding down, I'm already planning my spring projects – maybe something in lighter colors that won't show the dog hair quite as much!
